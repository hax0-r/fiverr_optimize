1. What is Char Dham Yatra?

The Char Dham Yatra is a pilgrimage tour to the four sacred places located in the foothills of the great Himalayas of the Indian state Uttarakhand. The main goal of this sacred journey is to cover four holy destinations known as Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.

These four destinations are located above the clouds in the Garhwal region of the Uttarakhand state of India. Gangotri and Badrinath are accessible by road, but Yamunotri and Kedarnath are not completely accessible by road. For Kedarnath, you will need to trek for 16 km whereas for Yamunotri you will need to trek for 6 km.

Completing these four ancient temples in one journey is referred to as Char Dham Yatra.

2. From where to start the Char Dham Yatra?

It is believed that beginning your Char Dham Yatra from Haridwar is the best option if you are planning to reach Uttarakhand by train. Because starting from Haridwar includes Haridwar itself followed by Rishikesh and other places and you will not need to explore Haridwar at the end of your tour. Another reason is that Haridwar is also known as the gateway to Holy Char Dham and starting from Haridwar makes the tour extra positive.

If you are planning to take a flight then you can plan your tour either from Delhi or Dehradun. The only difference is that both places are 250 km apart and you will need a minimum of 12 days if you start the tour from Delhi and a minimum of 10 days if you start your tour from Dehradun.

There are other time-saving options also like Char Dham yatra by helicopter within 2 days and 5 days.

3. Where is Yamunotri Dham located?

Yamunotri is where the acclaimed stream the Yamuna happens on the earth. This antiquated sanctuary is situated in the Uttarkashi area of Uttarakhand, which is encircled by mightly Himalayan pinnacles secured by white snow. The Yamunotri sanctuary is arranged in the Garhwal zone within the Uttarkashi region.

To arrive at this sanctuary you should arrive at Janki Chatti, which is open by motor vehicle, but from Janki Chatti to Yamunotri Temple you should travel for 5 km or you can likewise book a helicopter/cart to make your excursion simple.

Yamunotri is a wellspring of the Yamuna stream. Yamunotri Temple is arranged in the western locale of Garhwal Himalayas at a height of 3,291 meters (10,797 ft) in the Uttarkashi area, Uttarakhand. Yamunotri is normally the primary visit on the Char Dham yatra. An enormous number of fans visit this holy place during the long stretch from May to October.

The sanctuary is devoted to Goddess Yamuna and has a black marble icon of the goddess. The Yamunotri sanctuary is an entire day’s excursion from Uttarakhand’s fundamental towns eg. Rishikesh, Haridwar, or Dehradun. The genuine sanctuary is only accessible by a 13-kilometer (8.1 mi) journey from the town of Hanuman Chatti and a 6-kilometer (3.7 mi) stroll from Janki Chatti, with ponies or carts available for rent.

The climb from Hanuman Chatti to Yamunotri takes in perspectives on a few cascades.

4. Where is Gangotri Dham located?

Gangotri is one of Char Dhams of Uttarakhand, arranged on the banks of the heavenly Bhagirathi River. Gangotri is situated at an elevation of 3048 meters in the Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India.

Gangotri is arranged in the northern portion of the Uttarkashi District and near the Indo-Tibetan border. It is around 300 km from Dehradun, 250 km from Rishikesh, and 105 km from Uttarkashi.

The first Gangotri Temple was built by the Nepalese general “Amar Singh Thapa”. The stream is called Bhagirathi at the source and gets the name Ganga (the Ganges) from Devprayag onwards where it meets the Alaknanda. The root of the blessed stream is at Gaumukh, set in the Gangotri Glacier, and is a 19 km journey from Gangotri. The sanctuary is closed from Diwali day consistently and is reopened on Akshaya Tritiya.

During this time, the icon of the goddess is kept at Mukhba town, close to Harsil. Ceremonial obligations of the sanctuary are administered by the Semwal group of pujaris. These pujaris hail from Mukhba town.

5. Where is Kedarnath Dham located?

The sanctuary, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva, is supposed to be over 1,200 years old. It was built by “Adi Shankaracharya” and is among one of the 12 jyotirlingas in India.

A visit to the Kedarnath sanctuary is an integral part of the celebrated Char Dham Yatra in Uttarakhand. The yatra (journey) also includes visits to shrines like Gangotri, Yamunotri, and Badrinath. Each of the four holy places is located in the Garhwal division of Uttarakhand.

The Kedarnath Temple, located on the Garhwal Himalayan Range in the Rudraprayag District of Uttarakhand, India, is one of the loftiest and most sacred Hindu temples as it is a part of Chhota Char Dham Yatra in Uttarakhand. It is situated at a height of 3,583 meters.

The sanctuary isn’t directly accessible by road and must be reached by a 22 kilometers (14 mi) tough trip from Gaurikund. Horse and machine services are available to reach the temple.

6. Where is Badrinath Dham located?

Badrinath gets its name from Badri, a kind of wild berry that once grew there in abundance. Badrinath is the site of a temple that contains a shrine of Badrinath (one of the numerous names of the Hindu deity Vishnu) and has been a renowned pilgrimage center for over 2,000 years. The temple is believed to have been built by “Adi Shankaracharya”, a philosopher-saint of the eighth century. Badrinath Peak (23,420 feet [7,138 meters]) is 17 miles (27 km) west.

Badrinath is a Hindu holy town and a Nagar panchayat in Chamoli district in the state of Uttarakhand, India. It is situated at an elevation of 11,204 feet (3,415 meters), in the shadow of Nilkanth Peak and between Nar-Narayana Hills, Badrinath is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage situated on the banks of the Alaknanda river.

Badrinath is easily accessible by road. It is located 525 Km from Delhi and 296 Km from Rishikesh. There are regular buses operating to Badrinath from Delhi, Haridwar, and Rishikesh.

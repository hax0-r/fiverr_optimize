Char Dham Yatra From Delhi In 12 Days: The Divine Himalayan Dream Of Every Pilgrim

Embark on a spiritual adventure from the heart of India's capital to the sacred realms of Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.

The Char Dham Yatra is a sacred pilgrimage circuit in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, encompassing four revered Hindu shrines: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. Starting from Delhi, the journey takes devotees through breathtaking landscapes of the Himalayas, allowing them to seek spiritual solace and connect with their faith.


Each destination holds immense religious significance, with Yamunotri dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, Gangotri to Goddess Ganga, Kedarnath to Lord Shiva, and Badrinath to Lord Vishnu. Pilgrims embark on this transformative 12-day journey, facing challenging terrains, serene valleys, and the sacred rivers that originate from these divine abodes. The Char Dham Yatra is not just a physical expedition; it's a soul-stirring experience that blends devotion, nature, and the profound cultural heritage of the Himalayan region.

Significances of a Char Dham Yatra from Delhi:


The Char Dham Yatra holds immense significance for Hindus, as it involves visiting four sacred pilgrimage sites located in the Himalayan region of Uttarakhand, India. The four sites included in the Char Dham Yatra are:


1. Yamunotri: The source of the Yamuna River, located in the Garhwal Himalayas. The main attraction is the Yamunotri Temple dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, where devotees offer prayers and seek blessings.


2. Gangotri: The origin of the holy river Ganges, situated in the Uttarkashi district. The Gangotri Temple, dedicated to Goddess Ganga, is a revered pilgrimage site visited by thousands of devotees seeking purification and spiritual enlightenment.


3. Kedarnath: Home to the ancient Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. This temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (divine representations of Lord Shiva) and holds great significance among devotees who undertake rigorous journeys to seek Lord Shiva’s blessings.


4. Badrinath: The abode of Lord Vishnu, located in the Chamoli district. The Badrinath Temple is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is believed to be the holiest of all Vishnu temples. It is visited by devotees seeking salvation and spiritual liberation.


The significance of the Char Dham Yatra from Delhi lies in its spiritual and religious importance. It is believed that embarking on this sacred journey and paying homage to these four holy sites can cleanse one’s sins, purify the soul, and lead to spiritual enlightenment. Devotees undertake this arduous pilgrimage to seek blessings, attain inner peace, and strengthen their connection with the divine.


For many Hindus, the Char Dham Yatra is a once-in-a-lifetime journey that holds deep cultural, religious, and spiritual significance. It is not just a physical journey but also a profound spiritual experience that fosters devotion, self-reflection, and a deeper understanding of one’s faith. Additionally, the scenic beauty of the Himalayas adds to the allure of the journey, making it a memorable and transformative experience for pilgrims.

Trip Highlights

The Char Dham Yatra is a pilgrimage tour to the four sacred places located in the foothills of the great Himalayas of the Indian state Uttarakhand. The main goal of this sacred journey is to cover four holy destinations known as Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. These four destinations are located above the clouds in the Garhwal region of the Uttarakhand state of India. Gangotri and Badrinath are easily accessible by road, but Yamunotri and Kedarnath are not completely accessible by road.


For Kedarnath, you will need to trek for about 18 km/side whereas for Yamunotri you will need to trek for about for 7 km/side. Shared Chopper seats are available for Kedarnath for easy access but Yamunotri will have only have Doly/Pony/Pitthu options to bypass the trek. Kedarnath trekkers also find these Doly/Pony/Pitthu options when they start their trek at Gaurikund. Completing these four ancient temples in one journey is referred to as "Char Dham Yatra".

Char Dham Yatra From Delhi Tou itinerary

Day 1 DELHI - HARIDWAR (200 KM/06 HR)

Pick up from New Delhi railway station/airport and proceed straight to Haridwar. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in into the hotel and take some rest. In the evening explores Mansa Devi Temple and get blessed by participating in a live Ganga Aarti at Har ki Pauri. Post Aarti proceeds to explore Om Pul, Amrapur Ghar, Daksh Prajapati Temple, and Bharat Mata Temple. Post-exploration returns back to the hotel and is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Haridwar.

Day 2 HARIDWAR - BARKOT (200 KM/08 HR)

Morning at Haridwar. Get refreshed and refuel yourself with a heavy breakfast. Post breakfast proceed straight to Barkot via covering beautiful Kempty Falls at Mussoorie. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in into the hotel at Barkot. The evening you can take a nature trek near your hotel and is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Barkot.

Day 3 BARKOT - YAMUNOTRI - BARKOT (45 KM/03 HR + 6 KM/SIDE TREK)

Wake up early, get refreshed, and have a cup of tea/coffee and get some packed food for breakfast. The cab will drop you at Janki Chatti and you will need to trek ahead. To explore Yamunotri Dham, you will need to trek for 6 km per side from Janki Chatti. The full-day trek and meals on your own. Get blessed by having a darshan of the idol of Goddes Yamuna which is the source of the great river Yamuna, at Yamunotri temple in the mighty Himalayas. The afternoon treks back to Janki Chatti and boards the vehicle for Barkot. The evening returns back to the hotel and is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Barkot.

Day 4 BARKOT - UTTARKASHI (85 KM/04 HR)

Cosy morning, get relaxed well. Get refreshed and have your hot breakfast at the hotel. Post breakfast checks out from the hotel and proceeds to Uttarkashi. En-route explores the famous Vishwanath Temple. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in to the hotel. The evening enjoys nature/city walks around your hotel and is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Uttarkashi.

Day 5 UTTARKASHI - GANGOTRI - UTTARKASHI (100 KM/04 HR/SIDE)

Wake up early, get refreshed, and have your hot breakfast at the hotel. Post breakfast, board the vehicle and proceed to explore Gangotri Dham. En-route enjoys picturesque Harsil Valley, Bhagirathi River, and the most magnificent views of the snow-clad Himalayas. After Gangotri Darshan, returns back to Uttarkashi. Lunch midway. The evening returns back to the hotel at Uttarkashi. The evening enjoys nature/city walks around your hotel and is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Uttarkashi.

Day 6 UTTARKASHI - GUPTKASHI (220 KM/10 HR)

Wake up, get refreshed and have your hot breakfast at the hotel. Post breakfast checks out and proceeds to Guptkashi. En-route enjoys the beautiful valleys of Chamiyala, Ghansali, and Agastmuni. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in to the hotel. The evening enjoys nature/city walks around your hotel and is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Guptkashi.

Day 7 GUPTKASHI - GAURIKUND - KEDARNATH JI

Wake up early and get refreshed. There are two mediums to reach the Kedarnath temple ie by road and by air. Please find the below details for both mediums.


By Road: If you are planning to reach the Kedarnath temple by road then your cab will take you to Sonprayag. In the high season (May/June) there is much rush on this road from Guptkashi to Sonprayag which has a distance of 30 km. Sometimes this distance takes more than 03 hours to reach Sonprayag.


So, leaving early from Guptkashi for Sonprayag will be a good idea. From Sonprayag you will need to take local conveyance for Gaurikund, which is 05 km ahead of Sonprayag. Gaurikund is the base camp for the Kedarnath trek. You will do medical and biometric registration here and then proceed for the trek to Kedarnath. It is a 16 km trek which takes around 5 hours to complete the trek for a normal person. You will also have other options like Dolly/Ponny if you wish to skip the trekking.


By Air: You can also choose to explore Kedarnath by Helicopter services. Helicopter services are time-saving and give you an amazing birds-eye view of the snow-clad Himalayan peaks behind the Kedarnath temple. If you choose to access the Kedarnath temple by Heli services then the cab will take you to Helipad from where you will do all the formalities and will take off for Kedarnath.


Once you reach Kedarnath, get check-in into the hotel. Take some rest and explore the surroundings on your own. Having dinner and overnight stay at Kedarnath.

Day 8 KEDARNATH JI - SONPRAYAG - CHOPTA

Wake up early, get refreshed. Do the necessary shopping for the puja at Kedarnath temple. You can book a special pooja on additional charges. After having the Darshan of Lord Kedarthan and pooja ceremony back to the hotel. Pack your luggage and check out. Post check out have your breakfast at Kedarnath. Post breakfast treks down/fly down to Sonprayag/Guptkashi. From Sonpryag/Guptkashi board the cab and proceed straight to Chopta. Chopta is about 75 km from Sonprayag which will take approx 3 hours to reach Chopta. Lunch midway. En-route explores Omkareshwar Temple at Ukhimath and then straight to Chopta. The evening arrives, get check-in to the camps. The evening explores a beautiful sunset and enjoys light music at the camps. Having dinner and overnight stay at Chopta.

Day 9 CHOPTA - BADRINATH JI (180 KM/07 HR)

The morning investigates the stunning sunrise at Chopta. Post breakfast checks out and proceeds to Badrinath. En-route explores Gopinath Temple at Gopeshwar and Narsingh Temple at Joshimath. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in into the hotel. The evening is free for your leisure. You can explore the surroundings and Badrinath temple in the evening. Back to the hotel, having dinner and overnight stay at Badrinath Ji.

Day 10 BADRINATH JI - JOSHIMATH - DEVPRAYAG (205 KM/06 HR)

Wake up early, get refreshed, and do the necessary shopping for the pooja at Shri Badrinath Ji. Take a hot bath in a natural spring hot pond at Badrinath Ji, which is also known as Tapt Kund. After having the bath you can choose to stay in line or you can skip the line by applying for a special pooja. After having the darshan and pooja ceremony back to the hotel.


Have your hot breakfast at the hotel and check out. Post check out proceeds to explore Mana village. Investigating Bhim Pul, Vyas Gufa, and other gems of Mana. The afternoon proceeds to Rudraprayag. En-route explores Joshimath, Nandprayag, Vishnuprayag, Karnprayag, and Rudraprayag. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in to the hotel. Evening free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Rudraprayag.

Day 11 DEVPRAYAG - RISHIKESH (80 KM/02 HR)

Refuel yourself with a heavy breakfast at the hotel. Post breakfast checks out and proceeds to Rishikesh. En-route explores Laxman Jhula and lunch at Laxman Jhula. The afternoon arrives, get check-in at the hotel and relax for an hour. The evening explores Janki Setu, Geeta Bhawan, Parmarth Niketan, and Ram Jhula on your own. The evening takes you to Triveni Ghat to witness the live Ganga Aarti at Rishikesh. The late evening returns back to the hotel, having dinner and an overnight stay at Rishikesh.

Day 12 EXPERIENCE RISHIKESH - DELHI (230 KM/06 HR)

Wake up early in the morning and rejuvenate yourself through Yoga practices at the bank of the Holy Ganges. Back to the hotel, and have your hot and delicious breakfast at the hotel. Post breakfast proceeds to explore Janki Setu followed by Parmarth Niketan, Geeta Bhawan, and Ram Jhula on your feet. Have your lunch at Ram Jhula. Post-lunch proceeds straight to New Delhi for the drop. The evening drops at New Delhi railway station/airport. Tour ends.

Certainly! Here are answers to 20 frequently asked questions about the Char Dham Yatra from Delhi:

1. What is the Char Dham Yatra?

The Char Dham Yatra is a sacred pilgrimage journey to four significant Hindu temples located in the Himalayan region of Uttarakhand, India.

2. Which are the four pilgrimage sites included in the Char Dham Yatra?

The four pilgrimage sites are Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath.

3. How long does the Char Dham Yatra take from Delhi?

The Char Dham Yatra typically takes around 10 to 12 days from Delhi, depending on the mode of transportation and the duration spent at each site.

4. What is the best time to undertake the Char Dham Yatra from Delhi?

The best time for the Char Dham Yatra is generally between May and October when the weather is favorable and the pilgrimage sites are accessible.

5. How should I prepare for the Char Dham Yatra in terms of clothing and gear?

It's advisable to pack both warm and lightweight clothing, sturdy walking shoes, rain gear, sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses. Additionally, carry basic medical supplies and personal hygiene items.

6. Is the Char Dham Yatra suitable for all age groups?

While the journey can be physically demanding, people of all age groups can undertake the Char Dham Yatra with proper planning and precautions.

7. What are the transportation options available for the Char Dham Yatra from Delhi?

Travel options include private cars, buses, trains, and helicopters, with each offering its own advantages in terms of comfort, cost, and time.

8. Do I need to obtain any permits or passes for the Char Dham Yatra?

Permits or passes may be required for certain restricted areas or trekking routes, so it's essential to check the latest regulations before embarking on the journey.

9. What are the accommodation options along the Char Dham route?

Accommodation options include hotels, guesthouses, dharamshalas, and campsites available at various price points to suit different budgets.

10. Are there medical facilities available along the Char Dham route?

Yes, medical facilities are available at major towns and pilgrimage sites along the Char Dham route, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies.

11. What are the main attractions and points of interest at each Char Dham site?

Each site has its own significance, including temples, natural wonders, and scenic landscapes that attract pilgrims and tourists alike.

12. Are there any specific rituals or customs I should be aware of before visiting the Char Dham sites?

Yes, it's important to respect local customs and traditions, such as removing footwear before entering temples and dressing modestly.

13. How challenging is the trekking involved in the Char Dham Yatra?

Trekking routes vary in difficulty, but most are manageable with proper preparation and fitness levels. Helicopter services are also available for those who prefer to avoid trekking.

14. Are there any restrictions or guidelines for photography at the Char Dham sites?

Some temples may have restrictions on photography or videography inside the sanctum sanctorum, so it's essential to follow the guidelines and respect the religious sentiments.

15. What are the dining options available along the Char Dham route?

There are numerous eateries, restaurants, and roadside stalls offering a variety of vegetarian cuisine along the Char Dham route, catering to different tastes and preferences.

16. How can I ensure the safety of my belongings during the Char Dham Yatra?

It's advisable to keep valuables secure and carry essential documents with you at all times. Lockers or safe deposit facilities may be available at some accommodations.

17. Is it possible to hire guides or join organized tours for the Char Dham Yatra?

Yes, hiring local guides or joining organized tours can enhance your experience and provide valuable insights into the history, culture, and significance of the Char Dham sites.

18. What should I do in case of emergency situations during the Char Dham Yatra?

Stay calm and seek assistance from local authorities, tour guides, or fellow travelers. Keep emergency contact numbers handy and follow safety protocols.

19. Are there any cultural events or festivals that coincide with the Char Dham Yatra schedule?

Yes, several festivals and religious events are celebrated at the Char Dham sites throughout the year, offering unique opportunities to witness local traditions and rituals.

20. What are some recommended additional attractions or side trips to consider along the Char Dham route?

Depending on your interests and time available, you may consider visiting nearby attractions such as Hemkund Sahib, Valley of Flowers, or Auli for adventure activities like skiing and trekking.

the unknown facts about uttarakhand

Came into existence on 9th November 2000 after splitting up from Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand is named after a combination of two words where Uttara means North and Khand has the meaning of Land. The state comprises two regions which were previously called Kumaon and Garhwal. Kumaon was formerly known as Manaskhand and Garhwal which as Kedarkhanda and it is worthy to mention that both the regions are entirely opposite to each other when it comes to culture, food, way of living and linguistics, etc.

When it comes to amenities, Uttarakhand possesses Asia’s biggest tiger reservoir which is called Jim Corbett National Park and is well known for the high number of Tigers in the area.

Rajaji National Park is another flamboyant charm for visitors as it has made its distinguished place due to its occupation of Asian Elephants & Leopards. The integration of attractions including sacred places naturally blessed places & wonderful views is a joint reason for an upward trend in many local & international tourists every year.

Do Dham Yatra From Haridwar : Discover Spiritual Sanctuaries


As you embark on this Do dham yatra from Haridwar, you will not only witness the architectural grandeur of these ancient temples but also immerse yourself in the rich tapestry of Hindu mythology and traditions. The journey to Kedarnath and Badrinath is not merely a physical excursion but a soulful quest for divine blessings and spiritual enlightenment. Each step taken on this sacred path is imbued with devotion and reverence, as pilgrims surrender themselves to the divine presence that permeates the serene Himalayan landscapes.

Kedarnath


Kedarnath, located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. Furthermore, this ancient temple nestles amidst snow-capped peaks, pristine glaciers, and lush green valleys at an altitude of 11,755 feet above sea level. Pilgrims undertake a challenging trek or opt for a helicopter ride to reach the sacred shrine, where they seek blessings and offer prayers to Lord Shiva.


In Addition, besides its religious significance, Kedarnath earns renown for its breathtaking natural beauty and serene ambiance. Surrounded by towering peaks and the Mandakini River flowing nearby, the temple provides a perfect setting for meditation and introspection. Devotees often embark on the trek to Kedarnath seeking spiritual solace and enlightenment amidst the tranquil Himalayan landscapes.

Badrinath

In the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, Badrinath, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, dedicates itself to Lord Vishnu. Nestled amidst the scenic backdrop of the Neelkanth Peak and the Alaknanda River, the Badrinath Temple sits perched at an altitude of 10,279 feet above sea level. According to Hindu mythology, believers hold Badrinath to be the abode of Lord Vishnu, who meditated here for thousands of years.

Do Dham Yatra from Haridwar Tour Itinerary

Day 1 HARIDWAR - GUPTKASHI/RAMPUR (220 KM/08 HR)

Pick up from Haridwar in the morning and proceed straight to Rampur, Guptkashi via covering Rishikesh, Byasi, Devprayag, Srinagar, Rudraprayag, Agastmuni, and Guptkashi hill stations on the way. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in to the hotel. The evening is free for your leisure. Dinner and overnight stay at Rampur.

Day 2 GUPTKASHI/RAMPUR - GAURIKUND - KEDARNATH JI

Wake up early and get refreshed. There are two mediums to reach the Kedarnath temple i.e. by road and by air. Please ﬁnd the below details for both mediums.


By Road: If you are planning to reach the Kedarnath temple by road then your cab will take you upto the Sonprayag. In the high season (May/June) there is much rush on this road from Guptkashi to Sonprayag which has a distance of 30 km. Sometimes this distance takes more than 03 hours to reach Sonprayag. So, leaving early from Guptkashi for Sonprayag will be a good idea. From Sonprayag, you will need to take local conveyance for Gaurikund, which is 05 km ahead of Sonprayag. Gaurikund is the base camp for the Kedarnath trek. You will do medical and biometric registration here and then proceed for the trek to Kedarnath. It is a 16 km trek that takes around 5 hours to complete the trek for a normal person. You will also have other options like Dolly/Ponny if you wish to skip trekking.


By Air: You can also choose to explore Kedarnath by Helicopter services. Helicopter services are time-saving and give you an amazing bird-eye view of the snow-clad Himalayan peaks behind the Kedarnath temple. If you choose to access the Kedarnath temple by Heli services then the cab will take you to the Helipad from you will do all the formalities and will take off for the Kedarnath. Once you reach Kedarnath, get check-in into the hotel. Take some rest and explore your surroundings on your own. Having dinner and overnight stay at Kedarnath.

Day 3 KEDARNATH JI - SONPRAYAG - CHOPTA/UKHIMATH (75 KM/03 HR)

Wake up early, get refreshed. Do the necessary shopping for the puja at Kedarnath temple. You can book a special pooja on additional charges. After having the Darshan of Lord Kedarthan and pooja ceremony back to the hotel. Pack your luggage and check out. Post check out have your breakfast at Kedarnath. Post breakfast treks down/ﬂy down to Sonprayag/Guptkashi. From Sonpryag/Guptkashi board the cab and proceed straight to Chopta. Chopta is about 75 km from Sonprayag which will take approx 3 hours to reach Chopta. Lunch midway. En-route explores Omkareshwar temple mat Ukhimath then straight to Chopta. The evening arrives, get check-in into the camps. The evening explores the beautiful sunset and enjoys bonﬁres with light music at the camps. Dinner and overnight stay at Chopta.

Day 4 CHOPTA - GOPESHWAR - BADRINATH JI (180 KM/05 HR)

The morning investigates the stunning sunrise at Chopta. Post breakfast checks out and proceeds to Badrinath. En-route explores Gopeshwar, Joshimath, and Pipalkoti. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in into the hotel. The evening is free for your leisure. If time permits you can go to visit Mana village to make your last day relaxing. You can explore the surroundings and Badrinath temple in the evening. Back to the hotel, having dinner and overnight stay at Badrinath Ji.

Day 5 BADRINATH JI - HARIDWAR (310 KM/10 HR)

Wake up early, get refreshed, and do the necessary shopping for the pooja at Shri Badrinath Ji. Take a hot bath in a natural spring hot pond at Badrinath Ji, which is also known as Taptkund. After having the bath you can choose to stay in line or you can skip the line by applying for a special pooja. After having the darshan and pooja ceremony back to the hotel. Have your hot breakfast at the hotel and check out. Post check out proceeds to explore Mana village. Investigating Bhim Pul, Vyas Gufa, and other gems of Mana. The afternoon proceeds to Haridwar. En-route explores Joshimath, Nandprayag, Vishnuprayag, and Karnprayag, Dhari Devi Temple, Srinagar, Devprayag and Laxman Jhula. Lunch midway. En-route explores at Rishikesh. Lunch midway. The evening drops at Haridwar railway/bus station. Tour ends.

Here we have answered some frequently asked questions (FAQs) about a Do Dham Yatra from Haridwar:

1. What is the significance of Kedarnath and Badrinath in Hindu mythology?

Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Badrinath is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Both hold immense religious significance in Hindu mythology.

2. How do I reach Kedarnath and Badrinath from Haridwar?

You can reach Kedarnath and Badrinath from Haridwar by road. Regular buses and taxis are available. Helicopter services are also available for those who prefer an aerial view.

3. What are the best times to visit Kedarnath and Badrinath?

The best time to visit Kedarnath and Badrinath is during the summer months from May to June and the autumn months from September to October when the weather is pleasant for travel.

4. Are there any age or fitness restrictions for visiting these pilgrimage sites?

While there are no strict age restrictions, both Kedarnath and Badrinath involve some degree of trekking or walking. It's advisable to be in good health and consult a doctor before embarking on the journey.

5. What is the duration of the trek to Kedarnath temple, and is there an alternative mode of transportation?

The trek to Kedarnath temple usually takes around 14-16 hours. However, there are pony and palanquin services available for those who cannot trek the entire distance.

6. Can I visit Kedarnath and Badrinath in a single trip from Haridwar?

Yes, you can visit both Kedarnath and Badrinath in a single trip from Haridwar. Many tour packages offer combined visits to both these pilgrimage sites.

7. Are there accommodation options available near Kedarnath and Badrinath temples?

Yes, there are various accommodation options available near both temples, including guesthouses, hotels, and lodges.

8. What are the weather conditions like in Kedarnath and Badrinath during different seasons?

The weather in Kedarnath and Badrinath varies according to the seasons. Summers are generally pleasant, while winters can be extremely cold with heavy snowfall. It's advisable to check weather forecasts before planning your visit.

9. Are there any medical facilities available along the trekking route to Kedarnath and Badrinath?

Yes, there are medical facilities available at various points along the trekking routes to Kedarnath and Badrinath, including first aid stations and medical camps.

10. Are there any restrictions or guidelines for clothing or attire when visiting Kedarnath and Badrinath?

While there are no strict dress codes, it's advisable to dress modestly and wear appropriate clothing, especially when visiting the temples. Additionally, warm clothing is essential, especially during the colder months.

11. What are the dining options available near Kedarnath and Badrinath?

There are several eateries and restaurants available near Kedarnath and Badrinath offering a variety of vegetarian dishes. However, it's recommended to carry some snacks and water bottles during the journey.

12. Can I carry electronic devices such as cameras and mobile phones to Kedarnath and Badrinath?

Yes, you can carry electronic devices; however, it's advisable to carry spare batteries or power banks as charging facilities may be limited in some areas.

13. Are there any specific rituals or ceremonies that take place at Kedarnath and Badrinath?

Yes, various rituals and ceremonies are performed daily at the Kedarnath and Badrinath temples, including morning and evening prayers, Aarti, and special poojas during festivals.

14. Is there any wildlife or natural attractions to see along the trekking routes to Kedarnath and Badrinath?

Yes, the trekking routes to Kedarnath and Badrinath pass through scenic landscapes with opportunities to spot wildlife such as Himalayan birds, deer, and occasionally, even Himalayan bears.

15. Can I hire a guide or porter for the trek to Kedarnath and Badrinath?

Yes, local guides and porters are available for hire at the starting points of the trek. They can assist you with navigation, carrying luggage, and providing insights into the local culture and geography.

16. Are there any sacred ponds or lakes near Kedarnath and Badrinath that are worth visiting?

Yes, there are several sacred ponds and lakes near Kedarnath and Badrinath, including Vasuki Tal near Kedarnath and Tapt Kund near Badrinath, which are considered holy and frequented by pilgrims.

17. What safety precautions should I take while trekking to Kedarnath and Badrinath?

It's essential to stay hydrated, carry necessary medications, and be cautious of altitude sickness. Additionally, it's advisable to trek during daylight hours and follow the instructions of local guides.

18. Can I visit Kedarnath and Badrinath during the monsoon season?

While it's possible to visit during the monsoon season, it's advisable to check weather forecasts and road conditions as landslides and heavy rainfall may affect travel.

19. Are there any cultural festivals or events celebrated at Kedarnath and Badrinath?

Yes, various festivals such as Maha Shivaratri at Kedarnath and Badri-Kedar Utsav at Badrinath are celebrated with great fervor, attracting devotees from all over the country.

20. Are there any trekking or adventure activities available near Kedarnath and Badrinath for adventure enthusiasts?

Yes, apart from the pilgrimage, there are opportunities for trekking, camping, and adventure sports such as river rafting and mountain biking in the surrounding areas of Kedarnath and Badrinath.

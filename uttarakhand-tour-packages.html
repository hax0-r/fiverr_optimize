As per weather reports, the monsoon seasons begin from the 15th of June Uttarakhand to make the place verdant green and smoothing to impress all and sundry. 4. WHAT ARE THE DELICIOUS CUISINES OF UTTARAKHAND?

Taste the delicious dishes of Uttarakhand during your Uttarakhand tours. Top 03 mouth watering dishes from Uttarakhand are listed below.

1- DUBUKE/DUBUK

Dubuke also called as Dubuk in the local regions of Kumaon. It is a Kumaoni dish having delicious taste if made in the Kumaoni style. Preplanning is necessary to prepare this dish well.

How to prepare Dubuke/Dubuk?
Dubuke/Dubuk can be made by two pulses either Gahat or Bhatt. Gahat and Bhatt are the pulses which are easily available in the market. To prepare this dish you have to soak any one type of pulse in the water for overnight. Next morning pulse will be easy to grind. Grind the pulse in a mixture grinder, do not make a thin paste. Real taste comes when you grind it between two stone pieces by hand. After grinding, take an iron utensil to prepare this. Put some oil in the pot and heat it, add ingredients which you use while preparing a normal Daal. After adding all the ingredients add the blended pulse into the pot and add enough water to prepare it. Boil the mixture for at least 30 minutes or more. Keep mixing it at the intervals of every 10 minutes. Serve hot with rice, taste the rich taste.

2- BHATT KU CHUTKANI

Bhatt ki chutkani is very easy preparation which anyone can easily prepare. Basically, it is a Daal prepared in a different way.

How to prepare Bhatt ki chutkani?
To prepare Bhatt ki chutkani, you need the pulse named Bhatt. It is a small oval shaped pulse with dark black or brown color. To prepare this dish you have to fry the Bhatt pulse in the oil, fry the pulse until it comes to a golden brown. Fry chopped onion until it changes to a golden brown and adds chopped tomatoes in it. Now add ingredients and add the fried Bhatt pulse in it. Add enough water in it and boil it for at least 30 minutes. Serve hot with rice.

3- BATHUVE KA KAAPA

Bathuve ka kaapa is high of nutrients and prepared in the winters. Bathuva is a plant similar to Palak. Kaapa can also be made by Palak and mustard leaves but it tastes best with Bathuva.

How to prepare Kaapa?
Kaapa is a very easy dish to prepare for a first timer. To prepare this dish you need to boil the green leaves of Bathuva/Palak with water. After boiling it for at least 30 minutes remove water and make past in mixture of the leaves. Now, prepare it as a normal dish. Serve hot with rice. This delicious dish gives you rich nutrients.

5. WILDLIFE SANCTUARIES, AND NATIONAL PARKS IN UTTARAKHAND

Uttarakhand has a rich mixture of a wide range of flora and fauna. Most populate Tiger reservoir “Jim Corbett National Park” is a source of wildlife attraction. The state is fully surrounded by various type of floral species. Many types of flora and fauna species can be seen at this Northern slop destination called Uttarakhand. Have a look at the below wildlife sanctuaries, and national parks which you can explore through our Uttarakhand wildlife tour packages.

  1. Jim Corbett National Park: Located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand. Famous for rich no of Bengal Tigers.
  2. Rajaji National Park: Located in Haridwar district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Asian elephants and leopards.
  3. Nanda Devi National Park: Located in Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. Famous for musk deer, snow leopards, black and brown Himalayan bears.
  4. Valley Of Flowers National Park: Located in Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. An UNESCO world heritage site famous for musk deer, snow leopards, black & brown Himalayan bears, and various species of rich flora.
  5. Gangotri National Park: Located in Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand spread over 2390 sq km is the third largest national park of India. Famous for musk deer, snow leopard and over 150 bird species.
  6. Govind National Park: Located in Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand. Famous for musk deer, snow leopard, black & brown bears, and Himalayan tahr.
  7. Askot Musk Deer Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Pithoragarh district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan jungle cat, civet, barking deer, serow, goral and Himalayan brown bear.
  8. Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Almora district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan goral, leopard and over 200 bird species.
  9. Nandhaur Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand. Famous for tigers, leopards, Asian elephants, and sloth bears.
  10. Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Gopeshwar district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan snow leopards, musk deer, and yellow-throated marten.
  11. Sonanadi Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Asian elephants, deer, chitals, tigers, and sloth bears.
  12. Benog Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Dehradun district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan goat, leopards, bears, and panthers.

4. WHAT ARE THE DELICIOUS CUISINES OF UTTARAKHAND?

Taste the delicious dishes of Uttarakhand during your Uttarakhand tours. Top 03 mouth watering dishes from Uttarakhand are listed below.

1- DUBUKE/DUBUK

Dubuke also called as Dubuk in the local regions of Kumaon. It is a Kumaoni dish having delicious taste if made in the Kumaoni style. Preplanning is necessary to prepare this dish well.

How to prepare Dubuke/Dubuk?
Dubuke/Dubuk can be made by two pulses either Gahat or Bhatt. Gahat and Bhatt are the pulses which are easily available in the market. To prepare this dish you have to soak any one type of pulse in the water for overnight. Next morning pulse will be easy to grind. Grind the pulse in a mixture grinder, do not make a thin paste. Real taste comes when you grind it between two stone pieces by hand. After grinding, take an iron utensil to prepare this. Put some oil in the pot and heat it, add ingredients which you use while preparing a normal Daal. After adding all the ingredients add the blended pulse into the pot and add enough water to prepare it. Boil the mixture for at least 30 minutes or more. Keep mixing it at the intervals of every 10 minutes. Serve hot with rice, taste the rich taste.

2- BHATT KU CHUTKANI

Bhatt ki chutkani is very easy preparation which anyone can easily prepare. Basically, it is a Daal prepared in a different way.

How to prepare Bhatt ki chutkani?
To prepare Bhatt ki chutkani, you need the pulse named Bhatt. It is a small oval shaped pulse with dark black or brown color. To prepare this dish you have to fry the Bhatt pulse in the oil, fry the pulse until it comes to a golden brown. Fry chopped onion until it changes to a golden brown and adds chopped tomatoes in it. Now add ingredients and add the fried Bhatt pulse in it. Add enough water in it and boil it for at least 30 minutes. Serve hot with rice.

3- BATHUVE KA KAAPA

Bathuve ka kaapa is high of nutrients and prepared in the winters. Bathuva is a plant similar to Palak. Kaapa can also be made by Palak and mustard leaves but it tastes best with Bathuva.

How to prepare Kaapa?
Kaapa is a very easy dish to prepare for a first timer. To prepare this dish you need to boil the green leaves of Bathuva/Palak with water. After boiling it for at least 30 minutes remove water and make past in mixture of the leaves. Now, prepare it as a normal dish. Serve hot with rice. This delicious dish gives you rich nutrients.

5. WILDLIFE SANCTUARIES, AND NATIONAL PARKS IN UTTARAKHAND

Uttarakhand has a rich mixture of a wide range of flora and fauna. Most populate Tiger reservoir “Jim Corbett National Park” is a source of wildlife attraction. The state is fully surrounded by various type of floral species. Many types of flora and fauna species can be seen at this Northern slop destination called Uttarakhand. Have a look at the below wildlife sanctuaries, and national parks which you can explore through our Uttarakhand wildlife tour packages.

  1. Jim Corbett National Park: Located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand. Famous for rich no of Bengal Tigers.
  2. Rajaji National Park: Located in Haridwar district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Asian elephants and leopards.
  3. Nanda Devi National Park: Located in Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. Famous for musk deer, snow leopards, black and brown Himalayan bears.
  4. Valley Of Flowers National Park: Located in Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. An UNESCO world heritage site famous for musk deer, snow leopards, black & brown Himalayan bears, and various species of rich flora.
  5. Gangotri National Park: Located in Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand spread over 2390 sq km is the third largest national park of India. Famous for musk deer, snow leopard and over 150 bird species.
  6. Govind National Park: Located in Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand. Famous for musk deer, snow leopard, black & brown bears, and Himalayan tahr.
  7. Askot Musk Deer Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Pithoragarh district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan jungle cat, civet, barking deer, serow, goral and Himalayan brown bear.
  8. Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Almora district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan goral, leopard and over 200 bird species.
  9. Nandhaur Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand. Famous for tigers, leopards, Asian elephants, and sloth bears.
  10. Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Gopeshwar district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan snow leopards, musk deer, and yellow-throated marten.
  11. Sonanadi Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Nainital district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Asian elephants, deer, chitals, tigers, and sloth bears.
  12. Benog Wildlife Sanctuary: Located in Dehradun district of Uttarakhand. Famous for Himalayan goat, leopards, bears, and panthers.

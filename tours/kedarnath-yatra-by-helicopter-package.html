KEDARNATH TUNGNATH RISHIKESH YATRA TOUR ITINERARY

Day 1 DEHRADUN - GUPTKASHI (205 KM/08 HR)

Pick up from Dehradun in the morning and proceeds straight to Guptkashi via covering Byasi, Devprayag, Srinagar, Rudraprayag, Agastmuni, and Kashi Vishwanath Temple. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in to the hotel. The evening is free for your leisure. Having dinner and overnight stay at Sitapur.

Day 2 GUPTKASHI - PHATA - KEDARNATH JI (BY AIR)

Wake up early and get refreshed. There are two mediums to reach the Kedarnath temple i.e. by road and by air. Please ﬁnd the below details for both mediums. By Air: You can also choose to explore Kedarnath by Helicopter services. Helicopter services are time-saving and give you an amazing birds-eye view of the snow-clad Himalayan peaks behind the Kedarnath temple. If you choose to access the Kedarnath temple by Heli services then the cab will take you to Helipad from where you will do all the formalities and will take off for the Kedarnath. Once you reach Kedarnath, get check-in to the hotel/camps. Take some rest and explore your surroundings on your own. Having dinner and overnight stay at Kedarnath.

Day 3 KEDARNATH JI - PHATA - RISHIKESH (205 KM/08 HR)

Wake up early, get refreshed. Do the necessary shopping for the puja at Kedarnath temple. You can book a special pooja on additional charges. After having the Darshan of Lord Kedarthan and pooja ceremony back to the hotel. Pack your luggage and check out. Post check out have your breakfast at Kedarnath. Post breakfast treks down/ﬂy down to Phata/Sersi/Guptkashi. From Phata/Sersi/Guptkashi board the cab and proceeds straight to Rishikesh. Lunch midway. The evening arrives, get check-in into the hotel. Having dinner and overnight stay at Rishikesh.

Day 4 XPERIENCE RISHIKESH - DEHRADUN (25 KM/0.5 HR)

Wake up early in the morning and rejuvenate yourself through Yoga practices at the bank of the Holy Ganges. Back to the hotel, and have your hot and delicious breakfast at the hotel. Post breakfast proceeds to explore Janki Setu followed by Parmarth Niketan, Geeta Bhawan, and Ram Jhula on your feet. Have your lunch at Laxman Jhula. Post-lunch proceeds to Dehradun airport for the drop. The afternoon drops at Dehradun airport. Tour ends.

Certainly, here are 20 frequently asked questions (FAQs) about Kedarnath Yatra along with their answers:

1. What is the significance of Kedarnath in Hinduism?

Kedarnath is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in Hinduism. It is believed to be the place where Lord Shiva resides in the form of a jyotirlinga, symbolizing his cosmic light.

2. When is the best time to undertake the Kedarnath Yatra?

The best time to visit Kedarnath is during the summer months from May to June and then again in September to October. The weather during these times is pleasant and suitable for travel.

3. How long does the Kedarnath Yatra take?

The duration of the Kedarnath Yatra depends on the mode of transportation chosen. By helicopter, it typically takes 05N/06D, including travel time from Dehradun.

4. Is the Kedarnath Yatra physically demanding?

The Kedarnath Yatra involves trekking over mountainous terrain and can be physically demanding, especially for those not accustomed to high altitudes. However, helicopter services are available for those who prefer a more comfortable journey.

5. Are there accommodation facilities available during the Kedarnath Yatra?

Yes, there are various accommodation options available in Kedarnath, ranging from budget guesthouses to more luxurious hotels. It is advisable to book accommodation in advance, especially during peak seasons.

6. Are there medical facilities available along the route?

Yes, there are medical facilities available at various points along the route to Kedarnath. However, it is recommended to carry basic medical supplies and consult a doctor before undertaking the journey.

7. Can elderly people undertake the Kedarnath Yatra?

Yes, elderly people can undertake the Kedarnath Yatra, but it is advisable to consult a doctor before planning the trip. Helicopter services are available for those who may find the trek challenging.

8. Are there any restrictions for the Kedarnath Yatra?

Visitors are required to obtain permits for the Kedarnath Yatra, which are available at designated counters. Additionally, photography is restricted in certain areas, and visitors are expected to respect the sanctity of the pilgrimage site.

9. What clothing should I pack for the Kedarnath Yatra?

It is advisable to pack warm clothing, including jackets, sweaters, and thermal wear, as the weather in Kedarnath can be cold, especially during the early morning and evening hours.

10. Is there mobile network coverage available during the Kedarnath Yatra?

Mobile network coverage is limited in the mountainous regions surrounding Kedarnath. However, some providers may offer intermittent coverage at certain points along the route.

11. Can I undertake the Kedarnath Yatra without a guide?

While it is possible to undertake the Kedarnath Yatra without a guide, having a knowledgeable guide can enhance the experience by providing insights into the history and significance of the pilgrimage site.

12. Are there restroom facilities available along the route?

Yes, there are restroom facilities available at various points along the route to Kedarnath. However, visitors are advised to carry their own toilet paper and hand sanitizer.

13. Can I carry food and water during the Kedarnath Yatra?

Yes, it is advisable to carry sufficient food and water during the Kedarnath Yatra, as there may be limited options available along the route.

14. Is photography allowed at the Kedarnath Temple?

Photography is restricted within the inner sanctum of the Kedarnath Temple out of respect for its religious significance. However, visitors are usually allowed to take photographs in designated areas outside the temple.

15. Are there ATMs available in Kedarnath?

No, there are no ATMs available in Kedarnath. It is advisable to carry sufficient cash for expenses during the journey.

16. Can I visit other nearby attractions during the Kedarnath Yatra?

Yes, there are several nearby attractions worth visiting during the Kedarnath Yatra, including Bhairavnath Temple and Shankaracharya Samadhi.

17. Is the Kedarnath Temple open throughout the year?

No, the Kedarnath Temple is open to visitors only during certain months of the year, typically from April to November, depending on weather conditions.

18. Is the Kedarnath Yatra safe for solo travelers?

While the Kedarnath Yatra is generally safe, solo travelers are advised to exercise caution, especially while trekking in remote areas. It is advisable to travel with a group or hire a guide for added safety.

19. Can I undertake the Kedarnath Yatra during the monsoon season?

It is not recommended to undertake the Kedarnath Yatra during the monsoon season (July to August) due to the risk of landslides and heavy rainfall, which can make the trekking trails unsafe.

20. Can I perform special rituals at the Kedarnath Temple?

Yes, special rituals and pujas can be arranged at the Kedarnath Temple with prior arrangements. Devotees seeking to perform specific rituals should inquire at the temple office for further details.
